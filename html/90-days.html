Understanding the Inspection Process

Step 1: Self-inspection

Use the wear-and-tear card provided in your Lease-End Guide or download the Wear-and-Use Tool. Worried about mileage overage? Explore options to stay on track by using our Mileage Calculator.

Self-inspection is not considered an official measurement of wear and use, but you can use the information provided in the guide to get an idea of what type of damages may be revealed during your complimentary vehicle pre-inspection.

Helpful Resources:

  • Lease-End Guide
  • Wear-and-Use Tool
  • Excess Mileage Calculator
Step 2: Complimentary Pre-inspection

This is an important step to determine the amount of wear and use you have on your vehicle. By completing a pre-inspection, you'll learn what repairs are necessary to meet the guidelines you agreed to at the beginning of your lease. At 90 days from the end of your lease, you'll need to complete this step in order to have enough time to make repairs before turning in your vehicle. Should you choose not to repair damages, you'll be billed for these repairs after you turn in your vehicle.

Helpful Resources:

  • Schedule Pre-inspection
Step 3: Repairs and Maintenance

After you've completed your complimentary vehicle pre-inspection, you will be provided with a list of damages that need repair. We recommend having any necessary repairs completed at your dealership. Should you choose not to repair excess damages, you'll be billed for these repairs after you turn in your vehicle.
